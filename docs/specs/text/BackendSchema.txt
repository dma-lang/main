 Section 1

## Overview and scope
 This is the authoritative reference for every persistent data structure in the workbench. It defines the relational model, the enumerations, the control plane and per-version data plane, the story and carry-forward tables, the evidence, reasoning and governance tables, and the indexing strategy. It ships the DDL as schema.sql .
 Read it alongside the TRD, which explains why these choices were made; this document explains what to implement. Identifiers are referenced as FR-n and D-n against the revised PRD.
 System of record
 PostgreSQL is the system of record. Structure (foreign keys), lexical search ( tsvector ) and dense search ( pgvector ) all live in one engine, so a subcap, its relations, and its embedding are queried together.

 Section 2

## Modelling approach
 Six modelling decisions govern the schema.
 | Decision | What | Why

 | Two planes | A shared control plane and a per-version data plane. | Catalogue versions are isolated; cross-version facts live in the control plane.
 | v5 and v7 are segmented | Each version owns its own Postgres schema: cat_v5 and cat_v7, holding the identical table set. | Re-provisioning or reverting one version never touches the other; the active version is selected by schema name.
 | Stories are version-independent | The canonical Jira stories live once in the control plane and are related to each version through a bridge table, not duplicated. | One source of truth for delivery evidence; the same story can map to a v5 subcap and a v7 subcap.
 | Mapping-driven DDL | The data-plane schema and its relations are generated by the mapping studio. | New structure is data, not a migration; relations become foreign keys.
 | Provenance on AI rows | Every AI-derived row carries claim label, source tier, ERS and a reasoning-chain id. | Auditability is built into the model.
 | snake_case, typed enums | All names snake_case; controlled vocabularies are Postgres enums. | One source of truth for labels, tiers and statuses.

 The control-plane tables are created by versioned migrations. The data-plane tables are created at provisioning time from the confirmed mapping, which is why section 12 shows them as a canonical template within a cat_<version> schema, instantiated once per version. The relationship between stories and the catalogue is the subject of section 7.

 Section 4

## Sheet relationship map
 A catalogue workbook is not a flat table; it is roughly 25 sheets whose relationships to each other are the real model. This section makes those relationships explicit, because they drive provisioning (which relations become foreign keys and which become link tables) and the toggle cascade (what a subcap change propagates to).

### 4.1 The sheets and the role each plays
 The studio records every sheet and the canonical entity it populates, so it can reason about joins between sheets rather than loose columns. The hub is 2_Capability_Map ; nearly every other sheet references its Sub_Cap_ID .
 | Sheet(s) | Role | Canonical entity | Note

 | 2_Capability_Map | entity | subcap | The hub: every other sheet links back to its Sub_Cap_ID.
 | 4 / 8 / 10 / 11 | entity | l3_platform, agent, offering, data_product | Reusable entities referenced from many sheets; each carries a vendor.
 | 5_L4_Detailed_Features | entity | l4_feature | A three-way join: subcap x L3 platform x feature.
 | 6_Maturity_Descriptors | entity | maturity_descriptor | M1 to M5 rows owned by a subcap.
 | 7 / 9 | entity | product_component, construct | Catalogue components and platform constructs, linked to L3 / agents / features.
 | 12 / 13 / 15 | matrix | offering_subcap, data_product_subcap, theme_subcap | Many-to-many matrices with their own attributes (rationale, maturity lift).
 | 18_SubCap_Completeness_Profile | aggregate | subcap_completeness | Denormalised COUNTIF rollup per subcap; modelled as a view over the links.
 | 19_Toggle_Cascade_Simulation | control | cascade | Defines what a subcap toggle cascades to; modelled by the cascade flags on relations.
 | 21_VC_Mapping_PerSubcap | matrix | subcap_vcc | Subcap x value-chain cluster x subvertical.

### 4.3 How the relationships are stored and used
 These joins are persisted in control.relation_def (with cardinality, the realising sheet, and the cascade flags) and the sheets in control.catalogue_sheet . Provisioning reads them to build the foreign keys and link tables in the version schema; the cascade engine reads the rows where is_cascade is true to compute exactly what a subcap toggle affects (replacing the workbook’s sheet 19 simulation); and the carry-forward uses the version crosswalk sheet. Because the relationships are data, a new version with a different sheet layout is absorbed without a code change.
 The cascade is derived, not hardcoded
 Sheet 19 (Toggle Cascade Simulation) in the workbook enumerates, per subcap, which rows go inactive on a toggle. Here that is not a hardcoded list: it is computed by walking the cascade relationships in relation_def from the toggled subcap, so it stays correct as relationships change. The result is written to control.cascade_report .

 Section 6

## Control plane: identity, versions, mapping, relationships
 The control plane governs identity, catalogue versions, the schema-mapping configuration (FR-2), and ingest runs. The catalogue_sheet table records every sheet and the entity it populates; source_field_mapping records the field-level mapping; and relation_def records the sheet-to-sheet relationships (section 4) with their cardinality, the link sheet that realises them, and the cascade flags. Together these are the persisted record of how each version was brought online, and relation_def.cascaded_from records when a relation was propagated from another version.
 schema.sql · control plane sql
 -- ============ Control plane: identity, versions, mapping, ingest ============
CREATE TABLE control.users (
 uid text PRIMARY KEY,
 email text NOT NULL UNIQUE,
 is_admin boolean NOT NULL DEFAULT false,
 preferences jsonb NOT NULL DEFAULT '{}',
 created_at timestamptz NOT NULL DEFAULT now()
);

-- One row per catalogue version; schema_name points at its data-plane namespace (e.g. cat_v7).
CREATE TABLE control.catalogue_version (

 source_layout text, -- detected adapter
 schema_name text NOT NULL UNIQUE, -- 'cat_v5'
 status text NOT NULL DEFAULT 'draft', -- draft | provisioned | archived
 created_by text REFERENCES control.users(uid),

 notes text
);

CREATE TABLE control.ingest_run (
 run_id uuid PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
 version_id text REFERENCES control.catalogue_version(version_id),
 source text NOT NULL, -- workbook | jira | news | vendor | sow
 status text NOT NULL, -- running | ok | failed
 started_at timestamptz NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
 finished_at timestamptz,
 stats jsonb NOT NULL DEFAULT '{}'
);

-- Every sheet/tab in a version workbook, with the role it plays and the entity it maps to.
-- This is what lets the studio reason about sheet-to-sheet relationships rather than loose fields.
CREATE TABLE control.catalogue_sheet (
 sheet_id uuid PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
 version_id text NOT NULL REFERENCES control.catalogue_version(version_id),
 sheet_name text NOT NULL, -- '2_Capability_Map', '12_Offering_SubCap_Matrix', ...
 sheet_role sheet_role NOT NULL,
 maps_to_entity text, -- canonical entity this sheet populates
 row_count integer,
 UNIQUE (version_id, sheet_name)
);

-- The schema-mapping studio output: how each source field maps to the canonical model (FR-2).
CREATE TABLE control.source_field_mapping (
 mapping_id uuid PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
 version_id text NOT NULL REFERENCES control.catalogue_version(version_id),
 sheet_id uuid REFERENCES control.catalogue_sheet(sheet_id),
 source_field text NOT NULL,
 canonical_entity text, -- null when ignored
 canonical_field text, -- null when ignored / custom
 confidence numeric(4,3), -- 0..1 from auto-map
 status mapping_status NOT NULL,
 is_custom boolean NOT NULL DEFAULT false,
 created_at timestamptz NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
 UNIQUE (version_id, source_field)
);

-- Inter-sheet relationships, captured during mapping. Each row is a join between two sheets
-- (entities): the key columns, the cardinality, the link/matrix sheet that realises it, and
-- whether toggling the parent cascades to the child (and directly or indirectly). The cascade
-- engine reads these rows for a version to compute the dependency set; provisioning turns
-- one_to_many / many_to_one rows into foreign keys and many_to_many rows into link tables.
CREATE TABLE control.relation_def (
 relation_id uuid PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
 version_id text NOT NULL REFERENCES control.catalogue_version(version_id),
 from_entity text NOT NULL, -- e.g. subcap
 from_field text NOT NULL, -- e.g. subcap_id
 rel_type relation_type NOT NULL,
 to_entity text NOT NULL, -- e.g. offering
 to_field text NOT NULL,
 card cardinality NOT NULL,
 via_sheet text, -- the link/matrix sheet, e.g. 12_Offering_SubCap_Matrix
 is_cascade boolean NOT NULL DEFAULT false,
 cascade_kind cascade_kind NOT NULL DEFAULT 'none',
 cascaded_from uuid REFERENCES control.relation_def(relation_id),
 created_at timestamptz NOT NULL DEFAULT now()
);

-- Subcap-level crosswalk between catalogue versions (from each version _R1_Source_Reference sheet).
-- This is the deterministic bridge the story carry-forward uses to relate v5 subcaps to v7 subcaps.
CREATE TABLE control.version_crosswalk (
 crosswalk_id uuid PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
 from_version text NOT NULL REFERENCES control.catalogue_version(version_id),
 from_subcap text NOT NULL, -- base id in from_version (SV suffix stripped)
 to_version text NOT NULL REFERENCES control.catalogue_version(version_id),
 to_subcap text, -- null when a v5 subcap has no v7 successor
 note text,
 UNIQUE (from_version, from_subcap, to_version)
);

 Section 7

## Canonical stories, version segmentation, and the catalogue bridge
 This section answers three questions together: where the stories come from, and how roughly 14,406 of them relate to a catalogue that is segmented per version when they were only ever mapped against v5.

### 7.1 The canonical source
 The single authoritative story source is the Jira Full Story Catalog (the "Actual (Real Client)" export), held in control.story with its provenance ( source_system = jira , the export it came from, and the ingest time). It is 14,406 real stories today; the broader export runs to about 16,000 rows, of which the synthetic ones are flagged ( is_synthetic ) and excluded from analysis unless a trend surfaces them. The corpus supersedes the workbook story tabs and is stored once , not per version.

### 7.2 How a story relates to the catalogue
 Because v5 and v7 are separate schemas, a story cannot hold a single foreign key to a subcap. The relationship is materialised per version in control.story_subcap_carry : one row per (story, target version) giving the subcap the story maps to in that version, the similarity, the status, and how the link was made.

 For v5 , the version the stories were originally mapped to, the link is native ( via = native , similarity 1.0): the story’s sub_cap_id already is a v5 subcap once the subvertical suffix is normalised ( P3C1.8.CL2 → P3C1.8 ). For v7 and any later version, the link is carried forward by the script: the subcap-level control.version_crosswalk bridges v5 ids to v7 ids, and the catalogue-tuned embeddings confirm the two subcaps are similar before the link is kept.

### 7.3 Where the scripts fit
 Two deterministic, idempotent steps populate the bridge, both shipped as carry_forward_subcap_mappings.py alongside the PRD:

- Seed native links for the source version (v5): write one confirmed, via = native row per story.
- Carry forward to each target version (v7, then any future version): crosswalk first, embeddings to confirm, thresholds 0.86 (auto) and 0.70 (review); confirmed links are written, borderline ones are queued for a one-click admin confirm, and unmapped stories are never dropped. The step is keyed on (story_key, target_version) , so re-running it upserts and never duplicates.

);

## Integrity and provenance
 The schema encodes the product’s trust and resilience guarantees structurally, not by convention.
 | Guarantee | How the schema enforces it

 | Per-version isolation | A version’s catalogue lives in its own schema; re-provisioning or reverting it cannot corrupt another version.
 | Carry-forward idempotency | story_subcap_carry is unique on (story_key, target_version); a re-run upserts rather than duplicating.
 | Provenance on every AI row | claim_label, source_tier, ers and chain_id travel with suggestions, impacts, edges and transitions.
 | Gated commits | Nothing AI-proposed is written to the catalogue, the graph, or a suggestion-apply without a passing validation_gate_run.
 | Append-only audit | audit_log and export_manifest are insert-only for the app role; exports are HMAC-signed and immutable.
 | Relationships are data | Sheet-to-sheet joins live in relation_def; provisioning derives foreign keys and link tables, and the cascade is computed from them, not hardcoded.
 | Synthetic containment | story.is_synthetic plus trend_subcap.emergent are the only gate by which a synthetic story can be shown.
