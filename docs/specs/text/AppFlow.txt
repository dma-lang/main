 Section 1

## Overview and scope
 This document defines the navigation logic, the user journeys, the system-initiated flows, the cross-cutting state machines, and the per-page state contracts of the workbench. It is the single reference for "what should happen when..." for any interaction.
 It is sequenced for its readers: principles first, then the global state model engineering relies on, then the visual map, then the named journeys, then the system flows and cross-cutting behaviour, and finally a page-by-page state-contract appendix. Visual mockups live in the UI/UX brief; navigation and state live here.

### 1.1 The shape of the product
 The audience is a small, expert group doing high-stakes catalogue governance, so the flow optimises for trust, recoverability and traceability over novice onboarding. Every AI output links to its reasoning chain; every state-mutating action is reversible; every transition preserves the user’s filter context; every approval gate is explicit.

 Section 2

## Design principles
 Seven principles shape every flow in the product.
 | Principle | UX implication

 | Trust is the primary outcome | The reasoning viewer is reachable from every surface that shows AI output; the show-reasoning affordance is never buried.
 | Recognition beats recall | Filters, lenses and the active subvertical are always visible and carried between pages; users recognise context, they do not re-enter it.
 | Progressive disclosure | Surfaces lead with the headline (health, concentration, attention) and reveal depth on demand.
 | Reversibility | State-mutating actions show a what-will-change preview and are reversible; the cascade preview is the canonical pattern.
 | The catalogue is the gravity well | Most journeys end at a catalogue mutation or a synthesised artifact; the subcap deep dive is the common destination.
 | Failure is a designed state | Empty, degraded, low-confidence and unmapped states are designed surfaces with a next action, not error pages.
 | Mechanical governance | Commits pass deterministic gates; approvals are explicit; the audit trail is continuous from entry to mutation.

 Section 3

## Global state model
 Six pieces of state propagate across pages. They live client-side in a Zustand store and are serialized into the URL for deep links. On every page load the URL is parsed first; missing parameters fall back to the user’s saved preferences.
 | State object | Values | Effect

 | Active subvertical | One of the 9 (or all) | Filters every catalogue and evidence view to that subvertical.
 | Active pillar(s) | P1 to P4 (or all) | Scopes lenses, heatmaps and the explorer.
 | Claim-label filter | Any of FACT / INFERENCE / HYPOTHESIS / CEILING_ESTIMATE | Controls which AI assertions are shown.
 | Source-tier floor | T1 to T5 | Hides evidence below the chosen reliability tier.
 | Persona lens | A catalogue persona (or none) | Re-frames a subcap’s view around a role.
 | Scope context | Current subcap id or search term | Carried across drilldowns so a trail never loses its subject.

### 3.1 Propagation rule
 On every navigation the destination page reads the current state and applies it as its initial filter. The user does not re-filter on arrival. A deep link reproduces the exact filtered view.

### 3.2 The header surface (always visible)
 The header carries the Zennify mark and primary nav, the persona switcher, the theme toggle (light or dark), a live cost meter, a notification bell for open flags and suggestions, and the global filter controls for the six state objects. Settings and the admin schema-mapping surface are reached from here.
 Theme and cost are header state, not filters
 Theme is a saved preference and the cost meter is read-only telemetry; neither changes what data a page shows. The six objects above are the only state that filters content, which is why only they are URL-encoded.

 Section 4

## The app map
 The product is 27 pages across nine functional groups, plus login, the admin schema-mapping surface and Settings. Most journeys start at Mission Control and end either at a catalogue mutation (suggestion applied, status changed, version reverted) or at a published artifact (signed digest or DMA packet). The audit trail is continuous from entry to mutation.
 | Group | Surfaces

 | A · Explore | Mission control, Capability explorer, Subcap deep dive, Value chain atlas
 | B · Catalogue tools | Platform catalog, Use case explorer, Maturity heatmap, Knowledge graph (admin)
 | C · Project validation | SOW library, Story library, Project-subcap trace
 | D · Public intelligence | News watch, Trends monitor, AI suggestions, Benchmarks studio
 | E · Strategic synthesis | Quarterly strategic digest
 | F · Lifecycle & competition | Lifecycle manager, Vendor intelligence, Client journey atlas
 | G · Versioning & QA | Version timeline, Diff viewer, Change flags inbox, Validation gates log, QA & audit
 | H · Reasoning & RAG | Reasoning chain viewer, AI chat
 | I · Sandbox | What-if simulator
 | Access & admin | Login, Schema mapping (admin), Settings

 The full surface inventory, with objectives and contents, is in the UI/UX brief. This document is concerned with how a user moves between them and what state each consumes.

 Section 7

## Cross-cutting flows
 Behaviours that apply across every page rather than to one journey.
 | Flow | Behaviour

 | Reasoning access | Any surface showing AI output exposes a show-reasoning affordance that opens the reasoning chain viewer in context.
 | Filter preservation | The six state objects travel with every navigation and into deep links; arrival never resets context.
 | Approval gates | Any commit (suggestion apply, edge commit, status change) passes the deterministic gates; failures route to Change Flags.
 | Degraded mode | If a model provider is unavailable or over budget, surfaces show a degraded-mode banner and auto-route to the fallback model.
 | Empty state | Before any version is provisioned, the app routes an admin to upload and schema mapping; non-admins see a waiting state.
 | Synthetic visibility | Synthetic stories stay hidden until a trend flags their subcap as emergent, then appear labelled provisional.
