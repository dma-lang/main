 Section 1

## Purpose
 The Capability Intelligence Agent is an internal workbench that keeps Zennify’s four-pillar capability catalogue and its user-story corpus provably current, and lets pillar leads trace every catalogue assertion back to the evidence behind it.
 The system ingests two corpora: the capability catalogues and the user-story corpus . Catalogues arrive in versioned workbooks whose structures differ; the system reconciles them into one canonical model and, on upload, gives an admin direct control over how each version maps to that model. Ingestion is delivered in phases, beginning with the catalogue and story foundation and ending with continuous, automatic ingestion of new stories from Jira.

### Scope of this document
 This PRD defines the problem, the data foundation, the ingestion and schema-mapping model, the phased delivery plan, the AI strategy, the functional and non-functional requirements, and the product surfaces. The companion UI/UX brief holds the visual design system and high-fidelity mockups; this document references it rather than repeating it.
 Decision authority
 Where this PRD conflicts with any earlier document, the decision log in section 10 is authoritative.

 Section 2

## Context and the problem
 Zennify runs a financial-services consulting practice on a four-pillar capability catalogue maintained across four spreadsheet workbooks. The catalogue is the backbone of how the practice describes what it can deliver.
 Today the catalogue is governed manually. Statements of work in Drive, completed Jira stories, public regulator activity, vendor product launches, benchmark data and DMA assessments all carry signals that should drive the catalogue forward, but those signals are not systematically ingested, weighed or surfaced. The result is drift: the catalogue can fall behind delivery reality, and pillar leads cannot easily trace a catalogue assertion back to its evidence.

### Pain points

- Manual upkeep. Edits are hand-made; there is no record of why a change was made or what it cascaded into.
- Story signal is stranded. Thousands of delivered user stories describe what the practice actually builds, but they are not connected to the subcaps they prove out.
- Vendor and market shifts are invisible. When a platform vendor changes course, nothing maps that change to the affected subcaps.
- No provenance. An assertion in the catalogue cannot be traced to the evidence and reasoning behind it.

### Opportunity
 A workbench that ingests the catalogue workbooks and the story corpus, persists them once for every authorized user, then progressively layers delivery and public evidence, maps every signal to the subcaps it affects with an explainable reasoning trail, and proposes citation-grounded edits gated by human approval. This converts catalogue governance from manual to systematic and restores pillar-lead trust.

 Section 3

## Users, roles and goals

### Who uses it
 Primary users are internal consultants and pillar leads, roughly 10 to 30 power users. They own pillars, approve versioned edits, consume the quarterly digest, trace assertions back to evidence, and configure ingestion sources.
 Roles. Every authenticated Zennify user is a power user with full read access and the day-to-day approval actions. Admins , identified by a single is_admin flag, additionally get the knowledge graph, catalogue upload and source-registry edits, version revert, and on-demand digest generation. There is no finer-grained role model in v1.

### Ranked goals
 | Rank | Goal

 | 1 | Keep the catalogue provably current, with every assertion traceable to evidence.
 | 2 | Connect the story corpus to the subcaps it proves, and keep it current from Jira.
 | 3 | Surface vendor and market developments that should move the catalogue.
 | 4 | Turn delivery history into productization opportunities.
 | 5 | Hand a validated packet to the downstream DMA process.

### Non-goals for v1

- Client-facing use. The workbench is internal only.
- Role-based access beyond a single admin flag. Access is governed by one is_admin flag; finer-grained RBAC is out of scope for v1.
- Licensed-source ingestion (for example paid analyst feeds or technographics). Public sources only.
- A cross-pillar capability. De-scoped; see the decision log.

### 4.3 Two catalogue versions, reconciled
 Ingestion begins with two versions whose workbook structures are not the same.
 Catalogue v5

- Ten sheets per pillar, with inconsistent names and order across pillars (for example "Agentforce Offerings" vs "Agentforce Use Cases", "Technology Stack Matrix" vs "Technology Stack").
- Sheets: Overview, Capability Map, Sub-Vertical Matrix, Maturity Descriptors, Regulatory Frameworks, Product Catalog, Integration Map, Agentforce Offerings, Technology Stack, Gap Integration Log.
- No user-story catalogue. Stories appear only as gap-story counts and samples in the Gap Integration Log.
- Subcaps: P1 199, P2 292, P3 162, P4 187.
- Subcap id column is Sub-Cap ID ; capability is Cap ID / Capability .

 Catalogue v7

#### Canonical, 25 tabs per pillar

- 25 canonical tabs per pillar ( 1_Overview through 21_VC_Mapping_PerSubcap , plus Z1_QA_Gates , Z2_Plan_Revisions ), consistently named and ordered.
- Carries 3_User_Stories_Catalogue and 14_CrossPillar_Stories tabs, but for analysis both are superseded by the canonical Jira catalog, and the cross-pillar tab is not ingested.
- Ships reconciliation tabs: _R1_Source_Reference (v5 to v7 crosswalk) and _R2_Dropped_Stories (P4 also has _R3 ).
- Subcaps: P1 205, P2 292, P3 164, P4 190 (851).
- Subcap id column is Sub_Cap_ID ; snake_case throughout.

#### How the structures reconcile
 The two versions are reconciled into one canonical internal model rather than stored as rival schemas. On ingest the system:

- Detects the schema from the workbook signature (sheet names and header shape) and selects the right adapter, v5 or v7.
- Normalizes each sheet into canonical entities (subcap, story, maturity descriptor, L3 platform, L4 feature, offering, data product, agent, value-chain mapping).
- Bridges subcap lineage across versions using v7’s _R1_Source_Reference crosswalk, which already maps each v7 Sub_Cap_ID to its v5.0 original id and carries the old v5 fields. A subcap therefore keeps a continuous identity from v5 to v7.
- Records what could not be matched. Stories or rows that fail to match (v7 captures these in _R2_Dropped_Stories ; for example P3 has 488) are retained for review, never silently dropped.
- Versions every ingest. Each successful ingest is a snapshot; deviations from the canonical shape are flagged but do not block ingest.

 Why reconciliation matters
 v5 and v7 disagree on sheet names, sheet order, column names and, most importantly, on whether a story catalogue exists at all. Without a reconciliation layer the same subcap would appear as two unrelated records and the v5-era context would be lost. The canonical model plus the _R1 crosswalk keep one identity per subcap across versions.

### 4.4 Admin control over the schema (mapping studio)
 Auto-detection is a proposal, not a verdict. To stay resilient to however a future version structures its workbook, the admin who uploads a catalogue gets an interactive schema-mapping studio that opens as part of the upload and gives them full control over how that version maps to the canonical model. The studio is the setup step that makes each version’s persistent relational database functional.
 In the studio an admin can:

- Review and confirm the auto-mapping. Every source field is mapped to a canonical field with a confidence score and a status; the admin confirms the high-confidence matches and resolves the ones flagged for review.
- Override any mapping by picking a different canonical field, or remapping a field the auto-mapper guessed wrong.
- Add custom fields for columns that have no canonical home, so nothing in the source is silently discarded.
- Define relations from the UI (for example subcap addresses platform, subcap delivered-by story) that apply to this version of the catalogue.
- Cascade compatible relations to other versions. A relation defined for one version can be propagated to other catalogues where the participating fields exist and are type-compatible, so the admin does not redefine the same relation per version.
 Applying writes to the backend. When the admin applies the mapping, the configuration is written to the backend schema and the system provisions the persistent relational database for that catalogue version : tables for the canonical entities, the confirmed and custom fields as columns, and the defined relations as foreign keys. The mapping is stored per version and is re-runnable, so a correction re-provisions cleanly. This is what turns an uploaded workbook into a queryable, relational, persistent store.
 One studio, every version
 The studio runs for v5, for v7, and for any future version. Because mappings and relations are configuration rather than code, the product stays resilient to structural change in the catalogue without an engineering release.

### 4.5 Persistence
 Once a version’s mapping is applied and its database provisioned, the catalogue persists durably and is immediately available to every authenticated user. No re-upload is required across sessions, users or restarts. Re-ingesting a newer version creates a new versioned database rather than overwriting history.

### 4.6 Carrying story mappings forward across versions
 The canonical stories are mapped against v5, but the app analyses against the active version (v7 today, a later version tomorrow). A carry-forward correlation moves each story’s subcap mapping to the target version and confirms the two subcaps are actually similar before the link is maintained. This is the heart of the resilience model: delivery evidence is never stranded on an old version, and it is never silently attached to a subcap that has drifted in meaning.
 The correlation is deterministic first and semantic second:

- Normalize the id. Strip any subvertical suffix ( P3C1.8.CL2 → P3C1.8 + CL2 ) so the base subcap can be matched and the subvertical kept as context.
- Bridge with the crosswalk. Use the target version’s _R1_Source_Reference to map the v5 base id to its target id. This is exact and cheap.
- Confirm similarity. Embed both subcaps (name plus description) with the catalogue-tuned model and take cosine similarity. At or above 0.86 the link is carried forward automatically; between 0.70 and 0.86 it is flagged for a one-click admin confirmation; below 0.70 it is treated as unmapped.
- Fall back when there is no crosswalk hit. Find the nearest subcap in the target version semantically, and route it to review rather than guessing.
- Maintain the result. Confirmed links are written into the target version’s database; review and unmapped rows go to a queue and the Story carry-forward surface; nothing is dropped. The job is idempotent and re-runnable for every future version.
 carry_forward_subcap_mappings.py (core) python
 CONFIRM_AT = 0.86 # similarity >= CONFIRM_AT -> carry forward automatically
REVIEW_AT = 0.70 # REVIEW_AT <= sim < CONFIRM_AT -> flag for a one-click admin confirm
 # similarity < REVIEW_AT -> unmapped, propose nearest neighbour

ID_RE = re.compile(r"^(?P<base>P\d+C\d+(?:\.\d+)*?)(?:\.(?P<sv>[A-Z]{2}\d*))?$")

def normalize_id(sub_cap_id):
 # "P3C1.8.CL2" -> ("P3C1.8", "CL2"); keep the subvertical as context
 m = ID_RE.match(sub_cap_id.strip())
 return (m.group("base"), m.group("sv")) if m else (sub_cap_id.strip(), None)

def carry_one(story, source_cat, target_cat, crosswalk):
 base, sv = normalize_id(story.sub_cap_id)

 # 1) deterministic bridge first: the target version's _R1 crosswalk
 candidate = crosswalk.get(story.sub_cap_id) or crosswalk.get(base)
 via = "crosswalk" if candidate else None

 if candidate and candidate in target_cat:
 # 2) confirm the v5 subcap and its target counterpart really are similar
 sim = cosine(embed(subcap_text(source_cat, base)),
 embed(subcap_text(target_cat, candidate)))
 else:
 # 3) no crosswalk hit: nearest neighbour in the target, routed to review
 candidate, sim = nearest_subcap(subcap_text(source_cat, base), target_cat)
 via = "nearest_neighbour" if candidate else "none"

 if candidate and sim >= CONFIRM_AT:
 status = "confirmed"
 elif candidate and sim >= REVIEW_AT:
 status = "review"
 else:
 status = "unmapped"

 return CarryResult(story.story_key, story.sub_cap_id, base, sv or story.story_sv_code,
 candidate if status != "unmapped" else None,
 round(sim, 3), status, story.confidence_level, via or "none")

def carry_forward(source_version="v5", target_version="v7", include_synthetic=False):
 stories = load_canonical_stories() # the Jira Full Story Catalog
 if not include_synthetic: # synthetic excluded unless a trend flags it
 stories = [s for s in stories if not s.is_synthetic]
 source_cat = load_catalogue(source_version)
 target_cat = load_catalogue(target_version)
 crosswalk = load_crosswalk(target_version)
 return [carry_one(s, source_cat, target_cat, crosswalk) for s in stories]

 Why this is resilient
 The same routine runs v5 to v7 today and v7 to v8 next year. Exact crosswalks do the cheap work; embeddings only confirm; thresholds keep borderline cases in front of a human; and the full script ships alongside this document so it can be wired to the provisioned databases and run on each new version.

 Phase boundaries are about ingestion, not rewrites

### 5.3 Continuous Jira ingestion (Phase 3)
 The canonical Jira Full Story Catalog is a point-in-time export. Phase 3 keeps it live: the system ingests new completed stories from Jira continuously , extending the same canonical source rather than relying on a frozen file. For each new story it:

- pulls the issue and its fields on a schedule or webhook;
- matches it to subcaps with the catalogue-tuned model , assigning a match confidence and a short rationale;
- writes high-confidence matches into the story corpus, and routes low-confidence or unmatched stories to the change-flags inbox and a review queue rather than auto-merging them;
- records the match as an explainable reasoning trail, so a pillar lead can see why a story was linked to a subcap.

 Section 6

## AI strategy
 The AI strategy is retrieval-first and explainable. Models read the catalogue and the evidence and reason over them; they do not replace the catalogue’s own structure.

### 6.1 Retrieval-first, with catalogue-tuned matching
 Answers and matches are grounded in retrieved catalogue and evidence, not in model memory. The story-to-subcap and SOW-to-subcap matchers use a model tuned on the catalogue ontology : subcap names and descriptions, L4 features, and the existing story-to-subcap pairs. This gives the matcher a deeper understanding of what each subcap means at Zennify than an off-the-shelf model would have. The same embedding space powers the story carry-forward in section 4.6, so a subcap means the same thing whether it is being matched, retrieved, or carried between versions.
 The analysis set is the real Jira stories. Synthetic stories are not retrieved into analysis; they enter only when an external news or trend signal flags a subcap as emergent, and then they are clearly labelled as provisional.

### 6.2 Reasoning is shown in plain language
 Every AI output carries a claim label , its sources , and a reasoning trail that reads in plain language from the first line, with no opaque codes. The reasoning surface states the question, the ordered steps the model took, the evidence with readable source descriptions, any contradictions, the conclusion, an adversarial self-review, and the checks it ran shown as plain pass or needs-review results.
 | Claim label | Meaning

 | FACT | Directly supported by a trustworthy source.
 | INFERENCE | A reasonable conclusion from evidence.
 | HYPOTHESIS | A plausible idea that needs more evidence.
 | CEILING ESTIMATE | A best-case bound, not a confirmed value.

 Source trust is expressed as a readable tier (regulator down to vendor self-promotion) and an evidence reliability score that blends tier, recency, specificity and corroboration.

### 6.3 Where AI is deliberately not used

- Persona parsing is a deterministic split of the semicolon-delimited persona cell, not a model.
- The toggle cascade (deactivating a subcap and propagating to dependent rows) is deterministic.
- The validation gates are deterministic checks, not model judgement.

 Section 9

## Non-functional requirements

### 9.1 Authentication and access
 Sign-in is Firebase Auth with Google, restricted to the @zennify.com domain; the domain check fails closed, so non-staff never see product surface. Admin actions are gated by a single is_admin flag. There is no finer-grained RBAC in v1.

### 9.2 Persistence
 The catalogue and story corpus persist durably after the first authorized ingest, for every user, with no re-upload. New versions are new snapshots.

### 9.3 Cost discipline
 Cost is matched to leverage through three cadences: hourly evidence ingest, a monthly consultant cycle, and a quarterly digest. A visible cost meter and a monthly envelope keep spend in view; breaking changes carry extra, intentional friction.

### 9.4 Dark mode and accessibility
 Dark mode ships in v1 and every surface is verified in both themes. WCAG 2.1 AA is the floor.

### 9.5 Observability and reliability
 Each ingest run records timestamps, files attempted and loaded, schema deviations and per-sheet row counts. Source health (last poll, errors, items ingested) is surfaced. When a primary model is unavailable the system routes to a fallback and shows a degraded-mode flag.

### 9.6 Security and compliance
 Data-loss-prevention runs on every SOW that reaches a model. Audit logs are retained; audit-grade exports are signed and timestamped. Right-to-be-forgotten purges are admin-gated.
